❄ Ice Breakers

20 ready-to-use activities — online, movement & no prep. Click any card for full instructions and links.

20 activities
Mentimeter word cloud
📶 Online
🕐 5 min All levels
Students answer an open question on their phones and watch their words appear live on screen. The bigger the word, the more students said it.
Kahoot name quiz
📶 Online
🕐 8 min Elementary · Intermediate
Collect one fun fact from each student before class, then build a Kahoot where students race to guess which fact belongs to which classmate.
Padlet photo wall
📶 Online
🕐 10 min All levels
Each student posts a photo, emoji or GIF on a shared Padlet that represents their personality, weekend or home country.
Google Slides selfie gallery
📶 Online
🕐 10 min Intermediate · Advanced
Each student gets one slide on a shared deck to introduce themselves — photo or avatar, name, and three facts (two true, one false).
Wheel of Names spotlight
📶 Online
🕐 5 min All levels
Spin a digital wheel to pick a student at random, then reveal a fun question they must answer. Fast, fair and surprisingly exciting.
Jamboard emoji map
📶 Online
🕐 8 min Elementary · Intermediate
Students place a sticky note on a shared world map showing where they are from, with a fun emoji that represents their home country.
Human bingo
🏃 Movement
🕐 10–15 min All levels
Students circulate with a bingo grid of descriptions and find classmates who match each square. Forces real conversation and instant class mapping.
Four corners
🏃 Movement
🕐 8 min All levels
Each corner of the room is a choice or opinion. Teacher reads a question and students physically move to their answer. Zero prep, generates great discussion.
Line up!
🏃 Movement
🕐 8 min Elementary · Intermediate
Students arrange themselves in a silent line — no words allowed. Uses only gestures, fingers and miming. Brilliant when students don't yet know each other.
Find your pair
🏃 Movement
🕐 6 min All levels
Each student is given half of a pair — a word and definition, a country and capital, a question and answer — and must circulate to find their match.
Stand up if…
🏃 Movement
🕐 5 min All levels
Teacher reads statements and students stand if it applies to them. The fastest, simplest way to learn who's in the room and create shared experience.
Two truths and a lie — walk and talk
🏃 Movement
🕐 10 min Intermediate · Advanced
Classic two truths and a lie, made active by pairing students who rotate every 90 seconds. More conversations, more movement, more fun.
One word story
⚡ No prep
🕐 5 min Intermediate · Advanced
Students sit in a circle and build a story together one word at a time. Hilarious, spontaneous and completely unpredictable.
Desert island must-haves
⚡ No prep
🕐 8 min All levels
Students choose 3 things to take to a desert island and justify why. Sparks vocabulary, debate and personality reveals with no preparation.
Favourites lightning round
⚡ No prep
🕐 6 min Elementary
Fast-paced one-word answers fired around the room. Perfect for the first five minutes of an Elementary class — low stakes, high energy.
Would you rather
⚡ No prep
🕐 6 min All levels
Simple dilemma questions that get everyone talking. No materials needed — just a bank of fun either/or choices adaptable to any level.
Name acronym
⚡ No prep
🕐 8 min All levels
Each student creates an acronym from their name — each letter describes their personality. Creative, memorable and vocabulary-building.
The last thing I did
⚡ No prep
🕐 8 min Elementary · Intermediate
Students share the last interesting thing they did before camp. Perfect for past tense practice in an authentic, personal context.
10-second draw
⚡ No prep
🕐 8 min All levels
Students have exactly 10 seconds to draw something that represents them — no words. Universal, works across all languages and levels.
Common ground
⚡ No prep
🕐 10 min All levels
Groups race to find the most unusual thing they all share in common. The stranger the connection, the more points.
