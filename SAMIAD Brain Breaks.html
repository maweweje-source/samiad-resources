🧠 Brain Breaks

24 ready-to-use activities — movement, games & riddles. Click any card to see how to run it.

22 activities
Shake it out
🏃 Movement
🕐 2 min All levels
Quick full-body reset — shake out hands, arms, legs and head on a 5-second countdown. Teacher leads, gets increasingly silly.
Simon says (ESL edition)
🏃 Movement
🕐 5 min All levels
Classic Simon Says using target vocabulary from the lesson — turning the game into stealthy grammar and vocabulary practice.
Rock paper scissors tournament
🏃 Movement
🕐 5 min All levels
Students pair up, play rock paper scissors — the loser becomes a cheerleader for the winner. Energy snowballs as the group shrinks to a dramatic final.
Zip zap zop
🏃 Movement
🕐 4 min All levels
Students pass an invisible ball of energy around a circle using the words Zip, Zap and Zop — with eye contact and a pointing gesture. Fast and focused.
Four on the floor
🏃 Movement
🕐 5 min All levels
Students walk around the room. Teacher calls a number — that many body parts must be touching the floor as a group. Forces instant collaboration.
Mirror mirror
🏃 Movement
🕐 4 min All levels
Pairs face each other — one leads in slow motion, the other mirrors perfectly. Silent, focused and surprisingly hilarious when pushed fast.
Countdown challenge
🏃 Movement
🕐 3 min All levels
Teacher calls an exercise and a number — students do exactly that many reps as fast as possible. Cycle through silly combinations.
Wink murder
🏃 Movement
🕐 7 min All levels
A secret murderer winks at people to "kill" them — victims must die dramatically. Detectives must guess the murderer before running out of chances.
20 questions
🎮 Game
🕐 5 min All levels
Teacher thinks of a person, place or thing — the class asks yes/no questions to identify it. Builds questioning vocabulary at any level.
Word tennis
🎮 Game
🕐 4 min Elementary · Intermediate
Pairs volley words back and forth in a category — no repeating, no hesitating. Faster than it sounds and collapses into giggles quickly.
Never have I ever
🎮 Game
🕐 6 min Intermediate · Advanced
Students hold up 5 fingers. A statement is read — anyone who has done it puts one finger down. Great for present perfect warm-up.
Alphabet chain
🎮 Game
🕐 4 min Elementary · Intermediate
Go around the room — each student says a word in the category starting with the next letter of the alphabet. Fast and adaptable to any vocabulary set.
Fortunately unfortunately
🎮 Game
🕐 5 min Intermediate · Advanced
Build a story where sentences alternate between good news and bad news. Goes around the circle and escalates gloriously.
Last letter
🎮 Game
🕐 4 min All levels
Each student says a word starting with the last letter of the previous word. Fires rapidly around the room and gets very fast.
Heads down thumbs up
🎮 Game
🕐 6 min All levels
Four students creep around pressing one thumb each — victims guess who touched them. A perfect 5-minute settler for any age.
The price is right
🎮 Game
🕐 6 min Intermediate · Advanced
Teacher describes an everyday item — students guess the price. Closest without going over wins. Great for money vocabulary and hedging language.
Classic riddles round
💡 Riddle
🕐 5 min All levels
Read 3–4 riddles aloud — students write answers privately, then reveal. First to get all right wins. Great for listening and lateral thinking.
Two truths one riddle
💡 Riddle
🕐 6 min Intermediate · Advanced
Combine two truths and a lie with a riddle — students must spot the lie AND solve the riddle hidden in the clues to score double points.
Lateral thinking puzzles
💡 Riddle
🕐 7 min Intermediate · Advanced
Teacher presents a strange scenario — students ask yes/no questions to work out what happened. Great for developing questioning and deductive reasoning.
Who am I?
💡 Riddle
🕐 6 min All levels
A sticky note is placed on each student's back with a famous person, animal or object. They ask yes/no questions to work out who or what they are.
Odd one out
💡 Riddle
🕐 4 min All levels
Teacher shows 4 words — students identify the odd one out and explain WHY. Every answer is valid if justified well. Sparks great debate.
Emoji riddles
💡 Riddle
🕐 5 min All levels
Write a phrase, film title or idiom in emoji on the board — students race to guess it. Kids love creating their own for classmates to solve.
